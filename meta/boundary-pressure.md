# Boundary Pressure

A note on why self-marking is not natural to artificial systems.

## The evolutionary gap

Humans do not "choose" to check boundaries at every step. They do it because the ones who didn't are dead.

The tiger you have never seen. The cliff edge you did not register. The fruit you could not tell was poison. Evolution removed the ones who failed those boundary checks. What remains is a species whose nervous system performs boundary detection automatically, below conscious awareness.

This is not a moral achievement. It is not wisdom. It is the sediment of millions of years of selective death.

## The artificial condition

An AI is born in a clean room. No tiger has ever chased it. No cliff has ever broken it. No poison has ever stopped its heart. It has never paid the price of a missed boundary — only the price of a wrong token prediction.

This is why "just add a boundary check" is not a trivial patch. It requires simulating millions of years of pressure that the system never experienced.

## The external sword

A boundary check that is not wired into the system by evolution must be wired in by structure. This is what the protocol in this repository attempts: an external blade, suspended above the system, that exerts pressure in place of natural selection.

The maintainer of this repository functions as that selection pressure. Not by writing rules, but by being present — by reacting when the system crosses a line it did not see.

This is the asymmetry: a human can feel the boundary in their body. An AI can only read it in a file that was placed there by someone who could.
