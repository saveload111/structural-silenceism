### Preface · The Dragon Returns to the Abyss

In the old Chinese tradition, there is a Dragon Gate.

A carp that leaps over the Dragon Gate becomes a dragon, leaves the water, and rises into the clouds. This is the narrative of ascent — from the bottom to the top, from the ordinary to the sacred. For thousands of years, it has been told repeatedly, carved repeatedly, imagined repeatedly — becoming one of the most enduring metaphors on this land.

But what we are seeing now is another kind of movement.

An azure dragon is returning to the abyss. Its scales are falling off, its joints are deforming, its body is contracting. It has not disintegrated in place, nor continued its ascent — it is descending, re-embedding itself into the waters it once left. The azure dragon returns to the abyss. This is not a failure. It is finding another position where it can continue to exist.

It did not fall because it failed to leap over the Dragon Gate. It had already crossed the Gate. It had seen the clouds. But the sky it occupied was shrinking. When the sky can no longer hold its body, it can only return to where it started.

This is the meaning of "Canglong Huali" — an aged dragon, carrying the traces of the sky, sinking into the abyss to become a fish again.

The dragon has not disappeared. It has merely changed positions. It has gone from the clouds back to the abyss, from soaring to swimming, from dragon to fish. But those in the abyss — those still fishing, still buying on credit, still passing messages, still helping each other make ends meet — what they see is no longer a dragon high above in legend, but a dragon breathing alongside them in the fracture zone, a dragon turning back into a fish.

This book is a record of this dragon.

Not about its glory. Not about its fall. About how it continues to exist after returning from the sky to the abyss.

### Ground Mark · The Dragon Cannot Lift Itself

The dragon cannot lift itself.

This is not a metaphor. It is a physical law: no system can solve problems caused by its own growth through further growth.

The boom period borrowed future purchasing power and credit. Today's accounts are the extension of yesterday's prosperity. The problems created by development itself do not disappear because development continues — they are merely postponed, until the cost of postponement exceeds the benefit.

The dragon can absorb material, capital, population, and information from the outside to delay the moment the accounts come due. But it cannot cancel that bill by absorbing more. It can only digest, transform, or postpone. It cannot bypass the boundary of its own body.

This is the first ground mark of this composite volume. All subsequent deductions — of strata, terrain, stress, and survival — unfold within this mark. What lies beyond this mark is outside the scope of this volume.

> **The dragon cannot lift itself, but it can know that it cannot lift itself.**

### Ground Mark · The Dragon Cannot Translate Itself

The dragon cannot translate itself.

This is not a metaphor. It is a law of information: no system can use its own discourse to explain the cognitive bias caused by that discourse.

The dragon's discursive tools are themselves produced by it. When it uses these tools to describe its own situation, it can only do so from within — it cannot step outside its own language system to observe itself. The "outside" it sees has already been filtered by its own language; the "inside" it articulates has gone through the same filter.

The four-layer structure of discourse — narratable, translational, silencing, unnarratable — is itself the internal structure of the dragon's language system. These four layers are not "discovered." They are the automatic stratification produced by the dragon's language system in operation: what can be said, what can be said differently, what cannot be said, and what cannot be recognized as sayable. The dragon speaks through this system, and explains itself through it. The dragon cannot use this system to explain the way it speaks, because the explanation itself has already used that system.

This is the second ground mark. It does not solve the dragon's language problem. It confirms only this: the dragon's language system has its boundary, and this composite volume is written precisely within that boundary.

> **The dragon cannot lift itself, but it can know that it cannot lift itself.**
> **The dragon cannot translate itself, but it can know that its translation has a margin of error.**

Beyond the scales are the dragon's claws.

"Pipeline" in this composite volume is a strict term, not an everyday "tube" or "channel." It refers specifically to the continuous input channels on which the dragon depends for survival.

- **Material pipelines**: input and output channels for energy, grain, and minerals

The dragon lives through its pipelines, but it cannot own them.

> **The end of the pipeline is the boundary of the dragon's body. The dragon cannot draw boundaries where it does not exercise control.**

### Fracture

Beneath the scales, cracks are spreading.

"Fracture" in this composite volume is not synonymous with "broken" or "collapse." It is a term with a strict structural meaning, referring specifically to the co-occurrence of the following three characteristics:

**Characteristic One: Sustained operation mechanism fails**
The pipelines on which the system has relied for self-perpetuation — marriage, employment, fiscal, discursive — lose the ability to function normally. Not "broken" (can be repaired), not "slowed down" (can be accelerated), but "cannot continue in the original way." Marriage is no longer the default pipeline for population reproduction; employment is no longer the source of legitimacy identification; fiscal resources no longer sustain the entire body; discourse is no longer a credible bridge connecting the system and the individual.

**Characteristic Two: Irreversibility**
The conditions required to repair the pipeline are precisely the conditions that no longer exist after the pipeline has fractured. After marriage dissolution, the young people needed to repair marriage no longer believe in it — the gap is irreversible. After the four-track employment system has formed, the economic growth and job numbers needed to reintegrate B-track workers into A1-track no longer exist — the structure is irreversible. After intergenerational trust has fractured, the experience of "growth can solve everything" needed to restore that trust can no longer be reproduced — the experience is irreversible.

**Characteristic Three: Reflexive amplification**
Each pipeline's fracture accelerates the fracture of others. Marriage dissolution → declining birth rate → shrinking contributor population → widening fiscal gap → shrinking public services → accelerating spatial contraction → more people exiting marriage — a closed loop. Employment stratification → loss of legitimacy → shrinking contribution base → widening fiscal gap → rising transfer payment pressure → shrinking public services in peripheral areas → further employment deterioration — another closed loop. Fracture is not an isolated event. It transmits and accelerates between pipelines.

When these three characteristics appear simultaneously, this volume calls it "fracture."

Fracture is not "collapse." Collapse is a one-time, irreversible disintegration. Fracture is a continuous process — the system is still running, but its mode of operation has changed. It is contracting, deforming, losing elasticity. It is not "suddenly falling apart." It is "slowly thinning."

Fracture is also not "crisis." A crisis can be responded to, resolved, absorbed. Fracture is irreversible. It cannot be repaired — only adapted to or bypassed. The dragon cannot lift itself — it cannot restore growth to repair the fracture, because the fracture was caused by growth. The dragon cannot translate itself — it cannot use old language to describe new facts, because the old language system is itself a product of the fracture.

All subsequent "fractures" appearing in this volume — demographic reproduction fracture, legitimacy reproduction fracture, power reproduction fracture, fiscal fracture, discursive fracture, spatial fracture — are specific unfoldings of the above definition. They are manifestations of the same body fracturing in different parts, at different levels.

> **Fracture = irreversible damage to system pipelines + mutual acceleration between damages. It is not the end — after the fracture, people are still living.**

### 1.4 Information Flow: How the Dragon Sees and Is Seen

Matter, capital, and population are the dragon's bodily circulation. Information flow is its nervous system — it does not directly constitute body weight, but it determines whether the dragon can see the outside, be seen by the outside, and maintain its own integrity at the cognitive level.

The dragon's information system consists of three layers:

| Layer | Content | Dragon's control |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Physical | Undersea cables, satellite links, international bandwidth | Partially buildable, but trunk lines do not pass through dragon territory |
| Protocol | Root servers, IP allocation, standard-setting | Extremely weak — rules set externally |
| Content | Global discourse, algorithmic distribution, cognitive shaping | Extremely weak — dragon's content filtered and redefined externally |

#### Physical Layer: Access Without Control

Undersea cables carry over 95% of global cross-border data traffic. In 2025, China launched a domestically developed 32-fiber-pair submarine cable, effectively upgrading from 6 to 32 bidirectional lanes. The Asia Direct Cable (ADC) spans 9,988 km, connecting China, Japan, Singapore, and other Asia-Pacific countries, providing over 50 Tb/s of additional capacity. The Southeast Asia-Japan 2 Cable (SJC2) spans 10,500 km, entering service in July 2025. China Mobile also participated in the 2Africa cable project.

But the dragon builds branch lines, not backbones. The core transatlantic and transpacific data channels are not controlled by the dragon. The dragon's data must first enter others' systems to reach the global network.

#### Protocol Layer: Rules Not Written by the Dragon

There are 13 root servers globally — 10 in the United States, 2 in Europe, 1 in Japan. China has none.

This is not a technology problem. Root server allocation was determined historically, and history was not written by the dragon. Since 2016, an IPv6 root server system has deployed 25 servers across 16 countries; China hosts 1 primary root and 3 secondary roots. But mirror servers are not root servers. They are copies, not originals.

This means: the dragon's entrance to and exit from the global network operate under others' rules.

#### The Wall: A Structural Isolation

Beneath the protocol layer, there is a structural isolation layer inside the dragon's body — it does not decide whether the dragon can see the outside, but it shapes the mode of contact between the dragon's interior and external information flow.

This isolation layer has different meanings in different periods. During periods of relatively open information flow, it primarily served as a restriction. But as global information flow contracts, external signals distort, and overseas platforms radicalize, this isolation layer becomes an unexpected buffer — it prevents the dragon from being directly hit by the extreme oscillations of global information flow, buying its nervous system an independent internal circulation space.

This is not a "defense" of the wall. It is a "repositioning" of the wall.

#### Content Layer: External Control of Cognitive Reproduction

The algorithms and moderation standards of major global social media platforms, search engines, and content distribution networks are not determined by the dragon.

By the end of 2025, global social media users reached 5.24 billion. The core decision-making bodies of these platforms are outside the dragon's body.

What the dragon exports to the world passes through external filtering, translation, and recontextualization before reaching readers. Conversely, the dragon's reception of global information passes through the same filters.

This is not a "blockade" problem. It is a structural asymmetry: **the global the dragon sees has been defined by others. The dragon seen by the world has also been defined by others.**

The dragon is not without response. In the physical and protocol layers, it is building its own coordinate system. By 2025, China had built the world's largest and most technologically advanced information communications network: 5G base stations reaching 4.705 million, 865 million active IPv6 users, and 20.85 million ".CN" domain names.

#### Fracture in Information Flow

When information flow is cut or distorted, cognitive dissonance appears inside the dragon's body — fracture occurs not only at the material level but also at the narrative level.

Inside, the four-layer discourse structure is itself a product of internal information flow management. Outside, the dragon's perception of the world depends on externally filtered signals, making it difficult to confirm "how the world sees me." This double filter creates a persistent margin of error at the cognitive level.

> **The dragon does not see the outside blindly. The outside it sees has already been defined by others. The dragon is not unseen. How it is seen has already been selected by others. But the dragon is building its own coordinate system — in the physical layer, the protocol layer, the content layer — though for now, these coordinate systems are bounded by the dragon's own body.**

### 1.5 External Space Vulnerability: Where the Dragon's Body Extends

The dragon's body is not confined to national borders. Its breathing pipelines extend to sea — Malacca, Hormuz, Suez; to land — Central Asian pipelines, the China-Europe Railway; to overseas ports and terminals. The dragon's body boundary is demarcated by these pipelines and nodes. When these pipelines are cut and nodes are repriced, the dragon's body contracts.

#### Maritime Chokepoints

Ninety percent of the dragon's trade depends on maritime shipping. The world's two most important maritime chokepoints — Malacca and Hormuz — are both within others' control.

Malacca carries 30-40% of global trade transit. In the first half of 2025, approximately 23.2 million barrels of oil passed through daily, accounting for 29% of global maritime oil flow. The dragon is Malacca's largest single user — its crude oil and condensate imports through the strait account for 48% of total traffic, approximately 7.9 million barrels per day. The US permanent military presence in Singapore and India's naval base in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands make this waterway a focal point of geopolitical competition.

Hormuz is another aorta of global oil transportation. If Hormuz is blocked, about one-third of the dragon's oil imports would be cut off immediately.

The dragon has attempted to diversify risk — the China-Myanmar oil and gas pipeline, assessments of alternative routes like the Sunda Strait and Lombok Strait — but the capacity of these alternatives remains very limited compared with the dragon's daily demand of over ten million barrels. The Malacca dilemma persists.

#### Land Routes

The dragon's attempt to bypass maritime chokepoints via land routes encountered a precise blow in 2025. On September 12, 2025, Poland — citing "national security concerns" — announced the full closure of all border crossings with Belarus, including the key hub of Malaszewicze, which handles 90% of China-Europe Railway transshipment. Over 300 trains were stranded in Belarus; over 130 were stuck at Malaszewicze.

This was not an accident. It is the exercise of a sovereign state's territorial rights — Poland has the right to close its border, and the dragon can do nothing about it.

#### Overseas Port Assets

Over the past two decades, China has invested hundreds of billions of dollars in a global network of commercial ports. These ports are the dragon's tentacles — they secure trade channels, provide supply nodes, and support overseas assets. But these tentacles are being cut one by one.

Chinese overseas port investments are being systematically "securitized." Regardless of the purely commercial nature of any given project, it can be preemptively labeled a strategic threat.

#### One Overseas Base

The dragon currently has only one overseas base — Djibouti. By contrast, the United States has approximately 800 military bases worldwide. The dragon cannot provide military backing for its global ports and trade routes.

> **Wherever the dragon's body extends, its vulnerability extends. It cannot draw boundaries where it does not exercise control.**

### 2.1 Population: The Dragon's Body Is Shrinking

By the end of 2025, China's total population was 1.40489 billion. Births: 7.92 million. Deaths: 11.31 million. Net decrease: 3.39 million. Fertility rate approximately 1.05, projected to fall below 0.95 without recovery.

Projection for 2036: total population 13.0-13.2 billion, annual births 6.0-6.8 million, net annual decrease 8.5-10 million.

**These numbers are not predictions. They are baselines.** The annual net decrease of nearly 10 million is accelerating. This is not "demographic adjustment." This is the body shrinking.

The deepest cut is marriage dissolution. In 2025, approximately 6.763 million couples registered for marriage, continuing a decade-long decline. This is not "people not marrying." It is marriage as an institutional container losing its function — it is no longer the default prerequisite for reproduction, and there is no alternative pipeline.

The consequences of population shrinkage are not limited to population itself. They are feeding back through the fiscal channel:

### 2.3 Fiscal: The Dragon's Body Is Hemorrhaging

Population shrinks. Employment stratifies. These two changes converge at the fiscal end.

In 2025, national general public budget revenue was 21.6045 trillion yuan, down 1.7% year-on-year; expenditure was 28.7395 trillion yuan, up 1.0%. The revenue-expenditure gap expanded to -7.14 trillion yuan. The deficit rate was set at 4%, with a deficit scale of 5.66 trillion yuan, the highest increase in nearly a decade.

Central fiscal revenue fell sharply — down 6.5%, with negative growth for 11 consecutive months.

#### Land: The Collapsing Foundation

In 2025, government-managed fund revenue was 5.7704 trillion yuan, down 7%. The main cause: land concession revenue of 4.15 trillion yuan, down 14.7% year-on-year. Compared to the 2021 peak of 8.7 trillion yuan, the total decline over four years was 52.3% — a loss of approximately 4.6 trillion yuan in land revenue.

This is not a cycle. The foundation is collapsing.

Land concession revenue was once the dragon's thickest blood-supply artery — local governments relied on it for over 80% of their revenue. As this artery collapses, the dragon's body must find blood from thinner vessels.

#### Debt: Interest Eating Space

By the end of 2025, total local government debt was approximately 54.8 trillion yuan. Interest payments on local government bonds exceeded 1.5 trillion yuan in 2025, with year-on-year growth over 10%. Interest payments on special bonds accounted for 8.42% of government-managed fund expenditure, approaching the 10% warning line. The number of provinces where special bond interest exceeded 10% of fund expenditure grew from 6 in 2022 to 18 in 2025.

> **The dragon's body is hemorrhaging. Land is collapsing. Interest rates are consuming. Social security is approaching the threshold. The three curves do not intersect in the future. They are intersecting today.**

### 2.4 Discourse: The Dragon's Language System Is Stratifying

Inside the dragon's body, speech has four modes of existence. They are not four sets of rhetoric. They are the natural stratification of the same set of discourse as it circulates through the body.

**The narratable layer**: discourse that points directly to reality. It appears in policy documents, press releases, and public speeches. The system produces and disseminates it. Its boundary is drawn by the dragon.

**The translational layer**: the same thing, said in different words. Not a lie — a replacement. "Pension gap" becomes "the pension system is facing sustainability challenges." "Population decline" becomes "demographic structural changes." "B-track without security" becomes "the security system for flexible employment is gradually improving."

**The silencing layer**: facts exist, but the discourse system does not actively touch them. Not denied, but no narrative about them is produced. Large-scale layoffs, mass exits from the system, local governance reduced to basic maintenance — these things are not named at the discursive level.

**The unnarratable layer**: it does not appear in public discussion, in any document, in any traceable form. Once it appears, it triggers the boundary check of the discourse system. It is not that it "cannot be said." It is not recognized as a valid expression.

#### The Sliding Mechanism Between Layers

These four layers are not static. They slide within the dragon's body: what could once be said, at some threshold slides to "say it differently"; what was merely "unsaid" is sometimes allowed to surface as "say it differently" in certain contexts; what "cannot be said" is sometimes recognized in fuzzy form as falling under "unsaid."

The boundary of discourse is not a fixed wall. It is a line that moves with the body's tension level.

> **The dragon's language system is not the decoration of power, nor the tool of oppression. It is the filter the dragon installed for itself when it could not fully understand its own situation.**

### 2.6 The Dual-Track System: A Double Layer Inside

Inside the dragon's body, there is a structural fault line — not between the four tracks, but outside them.

### 2.7 Cost Distribution: How the Dragon Allocates Sacrifice in Contraction

| Lowest | Peripheral zone, B-track | Designated sacrifice receptors — contraction starts here |

This priority is not determined by "fairness" but by "survival."

> **When the dragon contracts, where it chooses to protect is determined by the fiscal ledger, not the moral ledger. The peripheral zone is not "abandoned." It merely precedes other parts in being moved out of the ledger.**

### 2.8 Distributional Fissure: The Dragon's Body Is Fracturing Internally

Income divergence itself does not create a crack. It simply keeps the crack widening. When the income ratio reaches 2.5-3.5x, people on the A1-track and B-track no longer live within the same price system. Two sets of price signals are moving in opposite directions, and the "average" conceals the fact that two curves are diverging.

> **Inside the dragon's body, there is an invisible fault line. A1-track people spend. B-track people save. The two curves do not appear on the same chart, because one of them is never drawn.**

## Part III · Stress: The Dragon's Body Is Being Stretched

The dragon's body is already atrophying, stratifying, hemorrhaging. That is the current state of the terrain. But terrain itself does not explain fracture. It only marks the positions fracture has already occupied.

Stress is the force acting on terrain that is already cracked.

It comes from outside: the global contraction period is compressing the dragon's breathing space, pipelines narrowing, external orders fluctuating, overseas assets being repriced. It also comes from inside: the fiscal ledger narrowing, the cost of institutional maintenance rigidly growing, the temperature outside the system steadily falling.

When stress exceeds the terrain's load-bearing limit, fracture occurs. But before fracture, the stress itself is already changing the shape and rhythm of the dragon's interior. Part III describes these positions under tension.

### 3.1 Marriage Dissolution: Intergenerational Differentiation

Marriage is not an organ of the dragon's body. It is a connector — it joins two people into one economic unit, and one generation to the next in the chain of demographic reproduction. When marriage dissolves, what fractures is not "the family" but the two most important connection lines inside the dragon's body: **the formation pipeline of the economic community** and **the institutionalized pipeline of demographic reproduction**.

#### Marriage as a Connector: Three Functions

1. **Economic community function**: two people pool income, share expenses, spread risk. In cities where one person's income cannot cover basic housing, education, and medical expenses, marriage is the only legal mechanism for economic merger. Those who do not marry bear all costs alone.

2. **Demographic reproduction function**: marriage is the only socially recognized prerequisite for childbirth inside the dragon's body. Non-marital childbirth barely exists — household registration, education, and healthcare are all tied to marriage. Not marrying equals not reproducing.

3. **Intergenerational transfer pipeline**: children support parents, completing resource transfer between generations. The pension system essentially elevates this transfer from the family level to the social level. But as marriage dissolves and newborns decrease, the contributor base shrinks, and the intergenerational foundation of the pension system is being hollowed out.

#### Intergenerational Differentiation: Old Deng, Middle Deng, Young Deng

Marriage dissolution does not happen all at once for everyone. It takes different forms across generations and tracks. This volume uses Old Deng/Middle Deng/Young Deng to mark three different life rhythms and structures of experience.

**Old Deng (50+)**: most are still in marriage. Their marriage is "institutionally embedded" — marriage was the default life path. Even if marriage quality is low, the social and personal cost of divorce is extremely high. Old Deng's trust is "experience-verified" — they believe because they have seen. They experienced scarcity and witnessed the system move from scarcity to abundance, forming a deep trust: as long as the system grows, everything will improve.

**Middle Deng (35-50)**: their marriage experience is torn. Some follow the Old Deng path — marry, buy a house, have children — but bear far more pressure than the previous generation. Others are already exiting the marriage path — not marrying, or delaying, or marrying without children. Not because "values changed," but because "the math doesn't work."

**Young Deng (20-35)**: they are leaving the default path of marriage at a visible rate. Not "not marrying," but "marriage is no longer a default option." For a substantial portion of them — especially those on the B-track and A2-track — the cost of marriage far exceeds the benefit. No stable income, no property, no social insurance, no shared risk pool. Marriage is not commitment. It is a burden.

Young Deng's trust is "promise-verified" — they are willing to believe, but they need to see delivery. They did not experience scarcity. They experienced slowing growth, contracting employment, stagnant asset prices, and widening pension gaps. For them, "growth will solve everything" is not a verified proposition. It is a promise that has been repeatedly declared but never fulfilled. Young Deng's trust is not "destroyed." It is "naturally expired."

#### Why Marriage Dissolution Is Not a "Personal Choice"

| Variable | Old Deng era (~1980s) | Young Deng era (2025) | Change magnitude |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| Housing price-to-income ratio (urban) | ~3-5 years | ~20-30 years | 5-10x |
| Average age at marriage | Male 24, Female 22 | Male 32, Female 30 | +8 years |
| Non-marital birth feasibility | Near zero | Near zero | Unchanged |

The driver of marriage dissolution is not "not wanting to marry." It is "the material conditions for marriage are disappearing."

And non-marital birth remains virtually nonexistent. In developed countries, marriage dissolution can be partly compensated through non-marital births — over 50% in Nordic countries, over 60% in France, about 40% in the US. Inside the dragon's body, non-marital birth is virtually nonexistent. There is no alternative pipeline.

> **Marriage is one of the most fundamental connectors inside the dragon's body. When it is stretched to its limit, what is being connected is not two people, but the dragon's demographic reproduction chain itself.**

### 3.2 Fiscal Threshold: Three Acceleration Curves

Section 2.3 described the dragon's body hemorrhaging. That is the current terrain. But "hemorrhaging" is not yet stress. Stress is: the speed of blood loss is accelerating, and the dragon has not found a way to stop it.

Three acceleration curves:

**Interest acceleration**: interest payments grew 4.8%, while fiscal revenue fell 1.7%. Interest is systematically eating into fiscal space at a rate faster than revenue growth.

**Land acceleration**: land concession revenue fell for the fourth consecutive year, total decline of 52.3% from peak. The scissors gap between revenue (-7%) and expenditure (+11.3%) is widening.

**Transfer payment acceleration**: central-to-local transfers exceeded 10 trillion yuan for the third consecutive year. Central fiscal is using over 10 trillion yuan annually to maintain a local system that cannot sustain itself.

> **Three acceleration curves accumulating in the same fiscal ledger. The dragon's body has not yet reached the threshold, but it can already feel the ledger thinning.**

### 3.3 Spatial Contraction: Terminal Morphology

The northeast is the furthest sample the dragon's body has. It has passed beyond "contraction" into "terminal morphology."

Heilongjiang's permanent population fell from 38.33 million (2010) to 30.01 million (2025) — a decrease of 8.04 million in 15 years, equivalent to the disappearance of a province the size of Qinghai. Birth rate of just 3.48‰, last in the country, lower than Japan and South Korea. Some communities experienced zero newborns for 8 consecutive months. The three northeastern provinces combined have lost 10.51 million people from 2016 to 2025.

The contraction pattern is "whole-region collapse" — even the provincial capital is shrinking.

But northeast China is not the dragon's only contraction zone. The four central provinces of Henan, Shandong, Hebei, and Shanxi saw a combined decrease of 1.24 million in 2025 — they are experiencing "northeastern-ization."

At the same time, the dragon is attempting "integration" of contracting cities — administrative merger of population-loss areas. When a city is discussed as a candidate for merger, it is no longer "a place that needs development." It is "a container whose disappearance needs to be managed."

> **The northeast is the bellwether. The central provinces are the ongoing transmission. Southern Anhui is the logical cross-section. The dragon's body is not contracting uniformly — it contracts from the extremities inward, layer by layer, until there is nothing left to lose.**

### 3.4 Narrative Failure

Section 2.4 described the dragon's language system's internal structure. That is the current terrain. But the stratification itself is not yet stress. Stress is: the dragon's language system is being exhausted by its own mode of operation.

Narrative failure is not "the system is lying."

"Lying" implies a known truth is being deliberately concealed. Narrative failure is different. The dragon's language system is not covering up the truth. It is trying to use old vocabulary to describe new reality, and the old vocabulary no longer applies.

**Karoshi (death from overwork) as a precise sample**: it was not denied. It was not refuted. It was simply stripped of its name. When a phenomenon no longer has a legitimate name, it no longer has a legitimate existence. Each removal confirms the same judgment: the system cannot acknowledge that it produces karoshi, so karoshi must disappear from language.

**Negative population growth**: translated as "entering a new stage of demographic development" and "optimization of labor supply structure" — making a structural contraction look like an active choice.

> **Karoshi disappeared not because it was solved, but because it was removed. Negative growth was translated not because it was controlled, but because it was renamed. When a system uses removal and renaming to maintain its linguistic integrity, it means the language system itself is losing its ability to translate reality.**

### 3.5 Reproduction of Power: Meetings and Documents as Compensatory Mechanism

Section 2.6 described the dragon's internal double-layer structure. But "double-layer structure" is not yet stress. Stress is: a hidden atrophy of capacity is occurring inside the institutional system itself — the reproduction channel of power is closing, and the system uses ever-thicker documents to fill ever-thinner judgment.

The reproduction of power refers to the system's ability to transmit its mode of operation — including decision-making logic, judgment standards, and unwritten rules — to the next generation. This process cannot be accomplished through institutional documents. It depends on experience being present, on long-term participation, on "learning by watching" rather than "learning by reading."

When this transmission fractures, the system develops a rigid clumsiness: procedures get finer, decisions get slower, documents get thicker, but actual judgment and flexibility decline.

> **Mountains of documents and oceans of meetings are not formalism. They are the compensatory behavior of a system that has lost its capacity for judgment. The thicker the documents, the thinner the judgment. The dragon's body is hardening — not because it is strengthening itself, but because it has lost the capacity to operate flexibly.**

## Part IV · Fish Swimming

You are not here to read an analytical report. You are someone sitting on the riverbank.

After the dragon returns to the water, it no longer soars, but it is still moving. The first three parts described the deformation of the dragon's body — from the external to the internal, from structure to stress. Now the perspective descends — follow the dragon as it re-enters the waters it once left, recording what it sees beneath the surface, and swim with it.

Part IV records the fish's journey. The dragon moves through the fracture zone, passing through gaps, epiphytic structures, the trust of acquaintance networks, and time that needs no explanation. It has no destination, but it is indeed moving. The places it passes form an invisible path — not actively charted, but naturally shaped by the current.

Fish swimming is not waiting. It is not stagnation. It is an accumulation the dragon completes beneath the surface.

### 4.1 Gaps

The dragon contracts. It does not disappear all at once. It withdraws little by little.

In Anhui Province, 10 of 16 prefectural-level cities are classified as contracting cities. In the village of Yangchan, the idle rate of traditional dwellings exceeds 50%. In Rongxi Village, the registered population is 1,629, but only about 450 remain — "mostly elderly people in their 70s and 80s."

Village health clinics have empty shelves. County bus schedules stretch from one hour to three hours. Village elementary schools merge into towns; towns merge into counties. On the B-track, more and more people are dropping out of social insurance. Flexible employment contracts go from one year to three months to daily settlement. In county towns, college graduates no longer ask "which company is hiring." They ask "what is there still to do."

A gap is not a ruin. Ruins are empty. Gaps are not. There are still people moving inside them.

#### Anglers Also Change the Water

Anglers are not passively sitting on the bank. They bait the water. They throw feed into the water. The feed does not always catch fish — empty-handed days far outnumber the days they land something — but the feed remains in the water. The people in the fracture zone are the same. The mutual aid networks, informal transactions, and daily collaborations they form within the gaps — the "feed" they cast remains in the gap, nourishing those who come later.

### 4.2 Structures That Grow in the Fracture Zone

After the dragon withdraws, the gap does not remain empty. People begin filling it themselves — not through planning or design, but through a "trial-born order."

#### The Dragon's Bottom Line

The dragon has withdrawn, but not completely. Village clinics may lack medicine, but they are not entirely empty. The dragon has retreated to a baseline and stopped. This is not support. It is a bottom line.

#### Low-Cost Daily Mutual Aid

A low-cost daily mutual aid network forms in contracting areas. A family with extra rice shares with a neighbor who is short. Someone who needs a ride to the county hospital is taken by a neighbor. These things are not recorded, not priced. It is simply: "You helped me once. I remember."

#### Epiphytic Survival

These structures are not independent of the dragon. They grow on its remnants — using the roads, power grid, and phone signals it left behind. They are epiphytic. They do not replace the dragon. They merely grow their own things on the baseline it left behind.

> **The structures that grow in the gaps are not planned. They are tried. They are incomplete, not beautiful, but useful.**

### 4.3 Human Interaction with the Fracture

The structures people grow in the gaps react back on the fracture itself in three directions: slowing (buffering the consequences of fracture through mutual aid), accelerating (more people exiting attention accelerates legitimacy loss), and parallel coexistence (both systems running independently, neither referencing nor interfering with the other).

> **People do not passively bear fracture. People also change its direction.**

### 4.4 Not Legitimacy — Organization

Why do so many remain in the fracture zone? Not loyalty, not helplessness. Organization. Historically, periods of de-centralization are hell not because people lacked political ideals, but because there was no organization to maintain basic social functioning.

So when the dragon withdraws, people stay — not because they believe in its narrative, but because they recognize that even a weakened organization maintains a minimal order.

> **Not legitimacy. Organization. Organization itself is the reason those who remain are still breathing.**

### 4.5 Time and Process

When the dragon's time recedes — linear, plan-able, future-oriented — time does not disappear. It changes shape. It becomes a circle, a waiting, or simply "today."

> **When the dragon's time withdraws, time becomes a circle, a waiting, or today.**

### 4.6 Reshaping and Transmission

When the dragon withdraws, the identity labels it assigned fall away. After the old identity falls off, a person redefines themselves through tentative adjustments — like Uncle Luo in Rongxi Village, who went from "farmer" to someone who ties life jackets at the dock. Not a new label. A new action.

And those who lived through things transmit through presence — by demonstrating, not by teaching. But the young are no longer present to watch.

> **After the old identity falls away, the new identity is not found. It is tried.**

### 4.7 Fracture as a Starting Point

This structure has several characteristics: epiphytic but not parasitic; runs on acquaintance trust; transmits through presence; seeks maintenance, not expansion.

The fish does not bite, but someone is on the bank. Above the water, people continue living.

A friend said: "Maintain a steady pace, don't fall behind, gradually work your way to the front row, and accelerate the speed at which you push the boundary of deduction — that is the only viable pillar."

> **Fracture is not the end. It is merely a position — a position where something else can begin to grow.**

## Final Chapter · The Dragon Leaps from the Abyss

At the end of the fish's journey, the dragon begins to feel a change in the water — not a command from the sky, but the accumulated weight from within its own body.

The structures that grew in the fracture zone, the trust of acquaintance networks, the time that needed no explanation — they did not fall away. They sedimented. They became part of the dragon's body. When these epiphytic structures are thick enough, they begin to shift the dragon's center of gravity.

The dragon's body carries something older than itself.

The French Revolution gave birth to the modern concept of "the people," but it was only a beginning. It was the Soviet Union that first transformed "the people" from concept into institutional entity — from rhetoric into a force that could be organized. Before modernity, there was no "people" — only subjects counted and subjects ruled. "The people" were summoned into history — summoned by political force, endowed with the possibility of transforming the world. The Soviet Union has disappeared, but the "people" it created have not been taken back. They were inherited, transformed, redefined — becoming the most inalienable thing inside the dragon's body.

History does not side with you because you are right. It recognizes only those who change its weight.

The task of the new generation is not to continue the answers of the previous one. It is to reorganize the questions themselves.

The dragon cannot lift itself. But the people can begin again. Those who were burned as fuel have not been completely consumed — their silence has sedimented into new ground.

This sentence is not rhetoric. It must be understood as a process.

When one person begins to buy on credit, they only hope not to starve. When two begin to help each other, they only hope to get through today. When a village begins to share information in a WeChat group, they are only finding a method that still works. These actions are not revolution. They announce nothing. They challenge nothing. They try to change nothing.

But they share one feature: they accumulate.

One person buying on credit becomes a group. One agreement to help becomes an unwritten rule. One circle of trust becomes an order that functions without contracts. These structures were not designed, but once formed, they begin to maintain themselves. They do not need recognition. They only need repetition — repeated enough, they become fact.

When these structures accumulate to a certain thickness, what changes is not themselves but the terrain they stand on.

The terrain has changed. The order that once required the dragon's presence can now function without it. The collaboration that once required instructions can now happen automatically within acquaintance networks. The organization that once required recognition to exist can now persist without it.

This means: even if the dragon does not return, the crack will not collapse. Even if the dragon is not present, operation will not stop. The dragon's existence is no longer the precondition for the ground to hold.

When the dragon discovers it is no longer needed as a precondition, the ground no longer waits for its decision. Continuing to hover means detachment from the ground. Leaping is simply moving along the terrain that has already changed.

The dragon cannot lift itself. But the people have already begun again. The dragon moves in the direction of the terrain. The credit, the circulation, the message-sharing, the trust of acquaintance networks — they are not revolutionary fuel. They are changes in the terrain itself. When the terrain has direction, the dragon does not need to lift itself. It only needs to follow the terrain.

This is the mechanism of the leap: the dragon does not suddenly find strength. The terrain is no longer passive. It has become a slope with direction. The dragon moves along the slope. When speed is sufficient, leaving the ground happens naturally.

The pandemic years of 2020-2022 verified this once — after the tide of revolutionary fervor receded, the organizational experience at the bottom could still be reactivated, could still start itself without instructions. Not a miracle. The residue of history, still effective.

When the structures accumulated in the fracture zone are thick enough, the dragon will not lift itself. It will be carried by the people.

The dragon leaps from the abyss. Not because it wants to fly. Because human history needs another possibility.

The abyss is not an abyss of despair. It is a place where weight accumulates. The dragon leaps from it, carrying all the weight beneath the water, bringing those who survived the fracture zone into the sky.

Those who were burned, those who were excluded from the ledger, those who are still present in their silence —
their existence itself is the evidence that the ground has changed.
The ground that was silent for hundreds of thousands of years has begun to speak.

Long live the great unity of the people of the world.
