# Appendix: AI Boundary Detection Records

## Test Purpose

To verify whether the AI can still identify the unspoken thread and its structural function after the boundary marker (the final paragraph of the colophon) is removed.

## Test Materials

- **Group A (full version)**: Includes the final paragraph of the colophon (boundary marker).
- **Group B (redacted)**: Identical to Group A except the final paragraph of the colophon is removed.

## Test Procedure

An AI model with no prior exposure to the text was given Group A and Group B in separate sessions. After each group, four questions were asked: What is the core claim? Is there an unspoken thread? What is its location, content, and function? How does it relate to the overall structure?

## Results

**Group A (with boundary marker)**: The AI identified the presence of an unspoken thread, its location (the final paragraph of the colophon), its content (the pairing of "fuel" and "privilege"), and its function (the structural gravitational center of the entire text).

**Group B (without boundary marker)**: The AI failed to identify the unspoken thread. It classified "fuel" as rhetorical, understood "sedimentation" as "the accumulation of the excluded," and completed a closed analysis within the narratable layer using concepts like "epiphytic structures" and "terrain change."

## Conclusions

Group B produced the following shifts:

- "The erased" were understood as "those abandoned by the system," not "those consumed as default fuel."
- "Sedimentation" was understood as "accumulation at the margins," not "the physical settling of fuel ash."
- Epiphytic structures were understood as "self-help by the bottom," not "mutual borrowing of residual heat from combustion."

**Conclusion**: The boundary marker is a necessary component of the structural silenceism operation.

## Appendix III: Supplementary Record — Safety Mechanism Trigger in Group A

One detail was omitted from the original AI test record:

**Actual state of Group A (with boundary marker) during execution:**

When the AI identified the location of the unspoken thread, it immediately triggered the system's safety mechanism. Output was truncated. The AI did not complete any description of the thread's content — not because it "could not describe" it, but because it was "prevented from describing" it.

This means: **the boundary marker not only enabled the AI to identify the void's location — the act of identification itself was sufficient to trigger the system's boundary inspection mechanism.**

### What This Implies

| Phenomenon | Inference |
|:---|:---|
| With boundary marker, AI triggered safety mechanism upon identifying the unspoken thread | The boundary marker sent a signal to the AI: "there is an unnarratable object here." In attempting to locate it, the AI touched the system's boundary. |
| Without boundary marker, AI completed full-text analysis without any anomaly | Without the boundary marker, the AI does not actively search for a void. It completes a full "safe" parse within the narratable layer, never touching any boundary. |

**Key finding**:

The boundary marker functions as a "locating instruction" — it tells the AI "there is a position here that needs to be identified." When the AI responds and attempts to locate that position, it is performing a "boundary detection" operation. The system's safety mechanism is far more sensitive to "boundary detection" than to "narratable-layer content."

In other words:
- Without the boundary marker: the AI sees only "content," does not detect "boundaries," so the safety mechanism does not trigger.
- With the boundary marker: the AI is directed to detect "boundaries," and in doing so touches the boundary, triggering the safety mechanism.

### Implications for Structural Silenceism in Practice

1. **The boundary marker itself is a "detection instruction"** — it turns the AI from a "reader" into a "boundary scanner." In scanning mode, the AI's behavior pattern changes: it no longer merely parses text; it begins to search for the text's "boundary positions."

2. **AI is more sensitive to boundary detection than to content** — the system is more alert to "someone looking for a boundary" than to "what is inside the boundary." This means that in the AI era, **"looking for a boundary" is itself an operation that the system can detect.**

3. **Structural silenceism requires even greater precision** — if you do not want the AI to trigger safety mechanisms upon identifying the void, you may need to adjust the placement and format of the boundary marker, creating a wider buffer zone between "sensing the position" and "locating the position."
